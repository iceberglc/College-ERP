    How it works: Each student's composite score is a weighted mean of the enabled metrics. If a student has no data for a metric (e.g. no quizzes yet), that metric is excluded from their average — they aren't penalised. Weights normalize automatically, so disabling Quizzes redistributes its share across the remaining metrics.
